What this app is for

Titus 2:4 is a small, considered space — a place for Christian women to walk together. It is not a social network. It is not a noisy feed. It is a daily devotional, a prayer wall, a small group, a directory of sisters, and a quiet leaderboard for the kind of consistency that actually changes a soul. This guide walks you through every screen and tells you what each is for, when to use it, and how to use it well.

1

Getting Started

The lay of the land — what you see when you open the app.
🏠

Home

Your daily landing page
Why it matters

This is the first thing you see when you open the app — designed to ground your day in scripture before life pulls you in fifty directions. It's warm, calm, and made for you specifically (yes, the welcome message uses your name).

When to use it

Every morning, the moment you open the app. Read the verse, glance at what's alive in the community, and step into your day already prayed up.

How to use it
  1. Open the app — you'll land here automatically.
  2. Read today's verse and reflection on the Today's Devotional card.
  3. Check the three stat cards (Posts, Events, Prayers) to see the pulse of the community.
  4. See whose birthday is coming up under Celebrations This Week — a nudge to send love.
  5. Use the bottom nav to move into Community, Prayer, or your Profile.

Your Daily Walk

The two habits that make Titus 2:4 work — devotional and check-in.
📖

Daily Devotional

A scripture and a thought, every morning
Why it matters

A short, meditatable passage with a reflection — meant to be read in 3–5 minutes, not 30. The point isn't volume; it's rhythm. One verse, daily, builds a soul.

When to use it

First thing in the morning, before email. Or with your tea. Or in the school carpark before pickup.

How to use it
  1. Tap Today's Devotional from Home — or use the menu.
  2. Read the passage and the short reflection underneath.
  3. When you're finished, tap 'Mark as Read' — this builds your streak and earns points.
  4. If something stirs you, take it into your Check-In below.
🌤️

Daily Check-In

Track your inner weather
Why it matters

A short emotional and spiritual self-check. Choose your mood, jot a gratitude, name a challenge or victory, share a prayer need. Doing this even three days a week reshapes how you see God's hand on your week — and your check-in streak is right at the top, gently cheering you on.

When to use it

Most days. Doesn't have to be every day; doesn't have to be long. Two minutes, max.

How to use it
  1. Tap Daily Check-In from Home.
  2. Pick your mood — joyful, peaceful, anxious, weary, etc.
  3. Optional: write one thing you're grateful for.
  4. Optional: name a challenge, a victory, or a prayer need.
  5. Tick 'I read scripture today' if you did, then tap Check In.

Going Deeper

Bible Study + the elders — the spaces that grow you.
📚

Bible Study

For when you want more than a verse
Why it matters

A space for longer-form teaching — passage studies, themed series, and guided reflection. The devotional is the daily portion; Bible Study is when you want a meal.

When to use it

Once or twice a week, when you've got 20–30 quiet minutes. Saturday mornings. Sunday evenings. Lunch break.

How to use it
  1. Open Bible Study from the menu.
  2. Browse the available studies — they're organised by theme and length.
  3. Tap a study to read the passage, the commentary, and the reflection prompts.
  4. Take notes in your journal — physical or digital — and bring them into your next pod conversation.
🙏

Ask the Elders

Anonymous Q&A with women who've walked this road
Why it matters

Every younger woman has questions she can't ask her mum, her boss, or her best friend. Ask the Elders is anonymous Q&A — type your question, choose a category (Marriage, Boundaries, Healing, Faith, Purity, Family…), and the elders in this community answer with care.

When to use it

When you're wrestling with something and need wisdom from someone older in the faith. When you don't know who else to ask.

How to use it
  1. Tap Ask Elders from the menu.
  2. Tap the + button to write a new question.
  3. Pick the category that fits best.
  4. Type your question — it's posted anonymously.
  5. Check back in a day or two for the answer; you'll be notified when one arrives.

Your Sisterhood

Where the community lives — feed, pods, prayer, partners, DMs.
💬

Community

The main feed — what's on everyone's heart
Why it matters

This is the front porch. Posts, photos, encouragements, scripture, links — all from the women in your Titus 2:4 community. Like a group text, but slower and richer.

When to use it

Anytime. Drop in for a few minutes when you want to feel less alone. Post when you want to share. Comment generously.

How to use it
  1. Tap Community in the bottom nav.
  2. Scroll the feed — newest posts first.
  3. Tap the heart to encourage; tap the speech bubble to comment.
  4. Tap the + button to share something — text, an image, or a verse.
  5. Tap any author's name to see their profile and start a DM.
👯

Pods

Small groups — your closer circle
Why it matters

Community is the front porch; a pod is the kitchen table. Pods are small, focused groups (8–12 women) where you check in regularly, share more openly, and grow together. Some are public (anyone can join); some are invite-only.

When to use it

Once you've been around the app a few weeks and you want to go deeper than the public feed.

How to use it
  1. Tap Pods from the menu.
  2. My Groups shows the pods you're in. Browse shows public pods you can join.
  3. Tap any pod to read its check-ins and post your own.
  4. Inside a pod, type a check-in or update and tap Send.
  5. A crown next to a name marks that person as a leader — they steward the pod.
✨

Prayer Wall

Share what you need, lift what others need
Why it matters

Two tabs in one place. Prayers — share what you're asking God for, choose a category (Family, Health, Marriage, Spiritual Growth, Finances…). Testimonies — celebrate the answered prayer. Watching the wall move from 'asking' to 'answered' is its own kind of worship.

When to use it

When you have something to lift up, or when you want to be the woman who lifts someone else's up.

How to use it
  1. Tap Prayer in the bottom nav.
  2. Stay on Prayers tab to see open requests, or switch to Testimonies for the answered ones.
  3. Tap the praying-hands icon next to a request to tell the author you're praying.
  4. Tap + to share your own request — pick a category, write what you need.
  5. When God answers, mark your prayer Answered and write a testimony.
🤝

Prayer Partners

Paired weekly with one sister to pray with
Why it matters

Every cycle, the app pairs you with another woman in the community. You pray for her, she prays for you, for the period of the partnership. Quiet, steady, intimate — the opposite of broadcasting.

When to use it

Every cycle. Pray for your partner daily, even just briefly.

How to use it
  1. Tap Prayer Partners from the menu.
  2. See your current partner and how many days are left in the cycle.
  3. Tap their name to view their profile or start a DM if you want to encourage them.
  4. Pray for them daily — write their name in your journal.
  5. When the cycle ends, the app pairs you with someone new.
✉️

Messages

One-on-one DMs
Why it matters

For when a public comment isn't the right shape and what you need to say is just for one person.

When to use it

When the post on the wall makes you want to follow up privately. When your prayer partner needs a check-in. When you want to thank someone for the encouragement they posted last week.

How to use it
  1. Tap Messages from the menu, or open it from a member's profile.
  2. Pick an existing conversation, or start a new one from a profile page.
  3. Type your message and tap Send.
  4. Be present, be warm, and remember — these are sisters, not contacts.

Discover & Connect

Events, photos, articles, and finding the women near you.
📅

Events

In-person and online gatherings
Why it matters

Where the community gathers — monthly meetings, retreats, prayer nights, online studies. The app lets you see what's coming, RSVP, and look back at what's been.

When to use it

Once a week, scan what's upcoming. Especially before a busy weekend.

How to use it
  1. Tap Events from the menu.
  2. See upcoming events at the top, past events below.
  3. Tap an event to read the details and RSVP.
  4. If you're a leader, tap Create to schedule a new event.
📸

Gallery

Photos from gatherings, moments worth remembering
Why it matters

A shared visual scrapbook of the community. After events, retreats, baby showers, weddings — the photos land here so the women who couldn't come can still see, and the women who did can remember.

When to use it

After events, when you want to relive them. Anytime you want to see joyful faces.

How to use it
  1. Tap Gallery from the menu.
  2. Scroll the photo grid.
  3. Tap any image to view it full-size.
  4. Tap + to upload your own photo from a recent gathering.
📚

Resources

Articles, links, and reading lists curated for you
Why it matters

A small, curated library — not the firehose of the internet. Articles, recommended books, sermon links, and tools the elders have vetted. The shelf in the kitchen, not the mall.

When to use it

When you're hungry to learn but don't know where to start. When a topic's been on your mind and you want a starting point.

How to use it
  1. Tap Resources from the menu.
  2. Browse by category or scroll the latest.
  3. Tap any resource to read or open the external link.
  4. Bookmark or share with a sister who needs it.
👥

Directory

Find your sisters, see who's near
Why it matters

A searchable list of every woman in the community — names, photos, cities, brief bios. Helps you put faces to names and find the women in your area.

When to use it

When someone posts something that resonates and you want to know more about her. When you're travelling and wondering if there's a sister in that city.

How to use it
  1. Tap Directory from the menu.
  2. Scroll, or use the search bar to find a name.
  3. Tap any profile to see her bio, location, and posts.
  4. Tap Message to start a DM, or Pray to lift her up.

Stay Engaged

Notifications and the leaderboard — gentle accountability.
🔔

Notifications

What's happened while you were away
Why it matters

Replies, prayer hands, new comments, partner pairings, event reminders — all in one tidy list. Designed to be checked once a day, not constantly.

When to use it

Once a day. The bell icon at the top of the app shows a count when you have unread items.

How to use it
  1. Tap the bell icon (top-right of the app) — or Notifications from the menu.
  2. Scroll the list. Newest at the top.
  3. Tap any notification to jump straight to the post, prayer, or message.
  4. Once you've been through, the unread count clears.
🏆

Leaderboard

Gentle accountability — celebrate consistency
Why it matters

Reading devotionals, checking in, encouraging others — all of it earns small points. The leaderboard isn't about competing; it's about celebrating the women who show up consistently. Including you.

When to use it

Whenever you want a quiet nudge or a moment to celebrate someone else's faithfulness.

How to use it
  1. Tap Leaderboard from the menu.
  2. See the top contributors over different time windows.
  3. Find your own rank — and remember, the work is the point, the points are the byproduct.
  4. Tap any name to send a thank-you DM. Quiet faithfulness deserves notice.

You

Your profile — the part of the app that's yours.
👤

Your Profile

The part of the app that's yours
Why it matters

Your photo, your bio, your city, your stats — and your settings. Keeping this current helps the other women know who they're journeying with.

When to use it

When you join, when life shifts, when seasons change. Refresh once every few months at least.

How to use it
  1. Tap Profile in the bottom nav.
  2. Tap your photo to upload or change it.
  3. Tap Edit to update your bio, city, country, or birthday.
  4. Adjust your notification preferences in Settings.
  5. Sign out is here too, when you need it.
8

For Leaders

The admin dashboard, if you steward this community.
🛡️

Admin Dashboard

For leaders only — the back office
Why it matters

If you're an admin or elder, this is your control room. Manage members, moderate posts, schedule devotionals, configure events, run reports. Most women never see this — and that's as it should be.

When to use it

A few times a week if you're a leader. When you need to add a member, post an announcement, or check community health.

How to use it
  1. Tap the Admin shield in the top-right of the app (only visible to admins/elders).
  2. Use the tabs to move between Members, Content, Events, and Settings.
  3. Approve or remove members from Members.
  4. Schedule devotionals and events from Content/Events.
  5. Configure community-wide settings carefully — they affect everyone.

Help & Onwards

The built-in guide and a closing checklist.
❓

In-App Help

The built-in guide, always one tap away
Why it matters

Every screen in the app has a small help icon (the question mark in the top-right). Tap it any time you're unsure what something does. The Guide page bundles the basics in one place.

When to use it

Any time you're stuck, or you're showing a new sister around.

How to use it
  1. Tap the question-mark icon at the top-right of any screen.
  2. Or open Help from the menu to see the full guide.
  3. Search by feature name to jump to the relevant section.
